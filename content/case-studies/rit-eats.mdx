<Problem title="Tigers gotta eat — so why is campus ordering still a chore?">

RIT EATS started from a premise anyone on campus recognizes: students are hungry, time is short, and ordering food around classes is more friction than it should be. Team Athens took that on in 2024 as **RIT EATS** — a campus food-ordering concept with its own identity, the *"Tigers gotta eat"* line, and the burger-forward branding in the title slide above.

That premise is as far as the surviving material goes. The specific problem the team set out to solve — what students do today, where it breaks down, and the baseline the design had to beat — isn't documented in anything that made it into this rebuild.

The research behind the concept is similarly undocumented: there's no surviving record of who the team talked to, what they observed about campus ordering, or which findings shaped the direction.

<Constraints title="What we know about the brief — and what we don't.">

Two things are firm. RIT EATS was a **team effort under the Team Athens banner**, and Palash's role was **Website Designer** — so the work sat closer to the brand, landing experience, and identity than to the ordering back-end. The title slide also fixes the visual direction: a confident orange-and-dark identity, the *"Tigers gotta eat"* tagline, and food-first imagery.

What shaped the work beyond that — the constraints the team designed around, the decisions they made deliberately, and what they chose to leave out — isn't captured in the surviving material.

Cassette illustrations · for the music tiles

Mixtapes, basically.

Each artist gets a stylized cassette tape with their name handwritten on the J-card label — like you wrote it yourself in marker on a tape you burned for someone. No portraits, no IP issues, more personal than a vinyl mockup.

At display size · 320×320

What makes the cassettes work: the metaphor is mixtape — these are tapes you would have made yourself. The label has your handwritten artist name in marker, a small subtitle that's a personal opinion ("the melody guy", "since high school"), and a tiny note at the bottom that's basically a love letter ("recorded from vinyl, w/ love"). The mono header text on the label feels like the standard cassette format. The spool wheels are visible through the window so it reads as a tape, not a generic box.
